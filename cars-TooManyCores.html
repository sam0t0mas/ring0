There was a time when a laptop was used for PowerPoint, email and pretending to work in meetings. Then Intel turned up with the Core Ultra 9 285HX and quietly replaced your office computer, gaming PC and home lab with a single silicon sledgehammer.

A Completely Senseless Number of Cores

Twenty-four cores. Eight Performance cores. Sixteen Efficiency cores. This isn't a laptop processor. It's what happens when a workstation escapes containment.

"Buying a 285HX laptop for Microsoft Word is like buying a Lamborghini to collect milk."

Gaming Has Become Ridiculous

Pair this thing with a high-end RTX GPU and you have enough compute power to generate frame rates, record gameplay, livestream, run Discord and still have resources left over to compile a Linux kernel.

The Mobile Datacentre

Virtual machines. Containers. Lab environments. Entire fake enterprises running inside VMware Workstation. The sort of people who get excited about that sentence will absolutely love this processor.

The Physics Problem

Officially it consumes 55 watts. Unofficially, laptop manufacturers seem to regard that as an opening negotiation. Under sustained load, cooling fans begin producing noises previously associated with small airports.
